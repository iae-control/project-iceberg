### Analysis: Why These Targets Fail

#### Missing Constants

- **E**: e = 2.71828... is transcendental and unrelated to trig/inverse-trig.
  cos(x) - arcsin(y) cannot produce e from algebraic combinations of
  0, EulerGamma, Glaisher within k<=7. The exponential function is needed.

#### Missing Unary Functions

The operator can naturally produce `Cos`, `ArcSin`, `Sin` (via identity),
`ArcCos` (complementary), and `Minus` (via subtraction). But it cannot
escape the trig/inverse-trig domain to reach:

- **ArcCosh**: Hyperbolic functions relate to exp/log via
  identities like sinh(x) = (e^x - e^(-x))/2. Without exp/log,
  these are unreachable.

- **ArcSinh**: Hyperbolic functions relate to exp/log via
  identities like sinh(x) = (e^x - e^(-x))/2. Without exp/log,
  these are unreachable.

- **ArcTan**: Although trigonometric, tangent is sin/cos ratio.
  Without division, tan cannot be composed from cos and arcsin.

- **ArcTanh**: Hyperbolic functions relate to exp/log via
  identities like sinh(x) = (e^x - e^(-x))/2. Without exp/log,
  these are unreachable.

- **Cosh**: Hyperbolic functions relate to exp/log via
  identities like sinh(x) = (e^x - e^(-x))/2. Without exp/log,

- **Exp**: Exponential/logarithmic functions are algebraically

- **Half**: Algebraic operations (1/x, x^2, sqrt, x/2) require

### Root Cause Summary

The 13/35 barrier exists because `cos(x) - arcsin(y)` is fundamentally
trapped in the **trig/inverse-trig + additive** domain:

1. **No exponential bridge**: Cannot reach exp/log, which blocks all
   hyperbolic functions and e.
2. **No multiplicative structure**: Subtraction alone cannot produce
   multiplication, division, or power operations.
3. **Trig-algebraic isolation**: Trigonometric functions are algebraically
   independent from exponential functions over the reals (though connected
   in the complex domain via Euler's formula, the verifier's k<=7 depth
   is insufficient to exploit this).

The 13 found targets are exactly those reachable within the
trig+additive closure: {cos, arcsin, sin, arccos, minus} for unary,
{plus, subtract} for binary, and {-1, 1, 2, pi} + base constants.
